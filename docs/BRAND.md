# Mnemo Med Brand System

Mnemo Med should feel like a clinical study cockpit: calm, precise, privacy-forward, and high-trust. The Pages presentation should communicate that it is a polished medical education product, not a generic prompt wrapper.

## Positioning

**One-line:** Source-locked study intelligence for medical school.

**Expanded:** Mnemo Med turns lecture notes, slides, professor wording, graphs, and weak topics into verified review sheets, Anki-ready cards, practice exams, and finals cram plans while keeping the course library local-first and offline-ready.

## Voice

- **Precise:** Say exactly what the app does and where evidence comes from.
- **Calm:** Avoid hype, fear, or overpromising clinical outcomes.
- **Academic integrity-first:** Study aid language, not shortcut language.
- **Privacy-forward:** Lead with local-first, no account, and offline PWA posture.

## Product proof points

Use these as short badges or trust bullets:

- Local-first
- Offline PWA
- Evidence-ranked
- No account required
- Source-locked prompts
- Professor wording preserved
- Anki-ready exports
- Weak-topic adaptive practice

## Page hierarchy

1. **Hero:** what it is, who it is for, and why source-locking matters.
2. **Trust posture:** local-first, no account, offline, academic integrity.
3. **Workflow:** course profile → source library → evidence ranking → outputs → review.
4. **Proof:** feature stats, screenshots, or GIFs.
5. **Install:** open Pages, install PWA, import/export backups.

## Design guardrails

- Prefer professional glyphs/SVG marks over emoji in primary navigation.
- Keep gradients restrained and clinical; avoid neon cyberpunk overload.
- Never imply the app replaces official course material or medical judgment.
- Any AI wording should be framed as optional, user-controlled, and evidence-grounded.
